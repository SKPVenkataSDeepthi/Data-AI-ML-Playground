## 📊 About the Challenge

The **Tanager Open Data Competition** by Planet Labs invites researchers and developers to explore the power of **hyperspectral satellite imagery** to solve real-world problems. ([Planet Learn][1])

Participants are encouraged to build:

* 📊 Impactful use cases
* 🧠 Technical analyses
* 💻 Code-driven solutions (notebooks, repos, models)

Winners get recognition and even influence which datasets become publicly available 🌍 ([Planet Learn][1])

## 🛰️ About Tanager Data

<p align="center">
  <img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/SKPVenkataSDeepthi/Data-AI-ML-Playground/main/Images/TANAGER%20COMPETETION.png" width="500"/>
</p>

Tanager satellites provide **hyperspectral imagery**—meaning they capture hundreds of spectral bands (not just RGB). ([Gee Community Catalog][2])

### Key Highlights:

* ~426 spectral bands (380–2500 nm)
* 30m spatial resolution
* Global coverage
* Applications across environment, agriculture, energy, and climate

👉 This allows detection of things invisible to the human eye—like vegetation health, minerals, and even gas emissions.
